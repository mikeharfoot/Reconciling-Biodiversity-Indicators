This infographic aims to illustrate how a range of biodiversity indicators (sometimes apparently inconsistent) arise from changes to species in a landscape being perturbed by land cover change and warming climate.

The infographic is comprised of two sections, (i) a hypothetical landscape, composed of three ecosystems initially, and (ii) a set of indicators of the biodiversity state of the landscape. A set of species exist in this landscape and have an affinity for a particular ecosystem type (indicated by the colour of the symbol) and a set of traits indicated by the symbol shape.

  • ★ Specialist to that ecosystem
  • ▲ Climate sensitive species
  • ● Generalist species
  • ■ Disturbance tolerant species
Different symbol colour and shape combinations represent different species, while the number of symbols represents the abundance of each species.
